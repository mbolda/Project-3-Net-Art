Did You Know The Number 39 Is Associated With Miku?

That's Why This Page Was Hidden On The 39th Miku On The Last Page

The reason lies in the pronunciation of the Japanese language. 39 is simply just a pun and it was soon picked up and used by Crypton Future Media, Inc., the creators of Hatsune Miku.
In Japanese, words can be made into numbers as a sort of shortcut. Numbers 1–10 have a kanji to them with a few different ways to read each of them. The number 3 is pronounced “Mi (み)” and 9 is pronounced “ku (く)” so 39 is pronounced “Miku”. That’s the main reason why it’s being used.
Also, there’s an alternate pronunciation where 3 is pronounced as “San (さん)” and 9 as “Kyuu (きゅう)” and, when combined, it says “Sankyuu” which is similar to “Thank you” in English. That’s another reason why it’s being used.
